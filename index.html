The Jam Line

Mobile App Design Project

Creating an app that brings exposure to Roller Derby and allows enthusiasts to stream and organize from their devices.

Project Summary:

Roller Derby is an underrepresented sport despite having multiple national and international league affiliations and a surprisingly large community. There aren’t any applications allowing skating and non-skating enthusiasts to organize and stay informed conveniently. This lack of access to content and exposure really impacts established teams as their older members relocate or retire from the flat track and their newer skaters and fans find it harder to be immersed in the game.

Scope: Refer to Case Study Outline for scope
Team: James Neal, Jasmin Pittito,Samantha Lambert
My Role: Research, Design, Ideation
Tools: Miro, Figma, Adobe XD, Invision, StoryboardThat

Challenge/Proposal

Given a recent revival in outdoor roller skating during the pandemic, a mobile application that allows fresh, seasoned, skating and non skating enthusiasts to grasp just how large their community is and can be, will solve this exposure dilemma. Users will be able to access and subscribe to local, national, and international streams, stay updated on team scoreboards and events, and shop for the latest team merch and skater gear. Team members will also be able to join their teams for a personalized experience.

Conclusion and Future Opportunities

We love helping the Roller Derby community thrive. We can’t wait for the opportunity to expand our features and make our app easily accessible to even more enthusiasts. For team members, we would like to add features that allow them to pay dues and organize from their team page. We believe that a chat feature could also bring new skaters closer, and encourage support within leagues.
